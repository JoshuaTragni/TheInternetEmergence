// 01 · Origins

Born in the Cold War

The Internet wasn't born in a garage. It was forged in Cold War anxiety ▸ when the U.S. feared a single Soviet strike could sever all military communication by destroying one central hub.

In 1969, DARPA activated ARPANET ▸ the world's first packet-switching network. Data could now travel multiple routes simultaneously, routing around destruction automatically.

What began as military infrastructure would, within three decades, rewire commerce, culture, politics, and personal identity across the entire planet.

// 02 · Timeline

Decades of Revolution

1969
ARPANET Goes Live

The first message sent between UCLA and Stanford crashes after two letters: "LO" ▸ an accidental abbreviation of "LOGIN."

1972
Email Invented

Ray Tomlinson introduces electronic mail and the now-iconic @ symbol, allowing messages to be routed to any user on any host. Overnight, communication is transformed.

1983
TCP/IP ▸ The Common Language

ARPANET adopts the Transmission Control Protocol / Internet Protocol, creating a universal standard that allows any network to connect to any other. The modern Internet is officially born.

1991
The World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee releases the WWW to the public. Hypertext links, browsers, and web pages make the Internet navigable for non-technical users for the first time.

1994–1999
The Dot-Com Boom

Amazon, eBay, Google explode onto the scene. Venture capital floods in, the Nasdaq soars 400%. A new economy seems to promise limitless growth.

2000–2001
The Dot-Com Crash

The bubble bursts. Over $5 trillion in market value evaporates. Thousands of companies fold. The survivors ▸ Google, Amazon, eBay ▸ emerge leaner and more dominant than ever.

2004–2007
Web 2.0 & Social Media

Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, and the iPhone launch in rapid succession. The Internet shifts from a place people visit to a place people live.

// 03 · The Revolution

Five Forces That Changed Everything

🌐

Globalization of Information

For the first time, anyone with a connection could access virtually all of recorded human knowledge ▸ instantly, at near-zero cost.

🛒

E-Commerce Revolution

Amazon began selling books in 1994; by 2024 it ships 1.6 million packages daily. Digital commerce reshaped retail, destroyed malls, and created a global marketplace without borders.

📡

Social Connectivity

Movements ▸ political, cultural, social ▸ could now organize and spread across the globe in hours, bypassing every traditional gatekeeper.

💼

New Economy & Work

The gig economy, remote work, and entirely new job categories (SEO specialist, UX designer, data scientist) emerged. Silicon Valley became the cultural and financial center of the new world.

🎵

Culture & Entertainment

Napster, Netflix, Spotify, and YouTube redefined how art is made, distributed, and monetized. Every established media industry was forced to transform or die.

🗳️

Politics & Democracy

The 2008 Obama campaign pioneered digital grassroots organizing. Social media later became a battleground for disinformation, foreign interference, and democratic discourse.

"The Internet is becoming the town square for the global village of tomorrow."
▸ Bill Gates, 1999
// 04 · Impact

A Double-Edged Revolution

The Internet Age delivered extraordinary gifts to humanity ▸ and introduced dangers its creators never envisioned.

Transformative Gains

  • Universal access to knowledge and education
  • Democratization of publishing and creativity
  • Global economic growth and new industries
  • Telemedicine and digital health tools
  • Instant cross-border communication
  • Coordination of humanitarian aid
  • Political organizing and civil rights movements

Critical Challenges

  • Disinformation and "post-truth" politics
  • Mass surveillance by governments & corporations
  • Cybercrime and ransomware attacks
  • Mental health crisis ▸ especially among teens
  • Job displacement through automation
  • Digital divide between rich and poor nations
  • Erosion of privacy and personal data rights
"We shape our tools, and thereafter our tools shape us."
▸ Marshall McLuhan
